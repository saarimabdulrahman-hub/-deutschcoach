# Zahlen und Farben — Numbers and Colors

## Numbers 1-10

Let's count in German:

| Number | German | Number | German |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 1 | eins | 6 | sechs |
| 2 | zwei | 7 | sieben |
| 3 | drei | 8 | acht |
| 4 | vier | 9 | neun |
| 5 | fünf | 10 | zehn |

**Tip:** Practice counting forward and backward. In German phone numbers, digits are read individually.

## Colors

| Color | German | Color | German |
|-------|--------|-------|--------|
| Red | rot | Yellow | gelb |
| Blue | blau | Black | schwarz |
| Green | grün | White | weiß |

## Simple Sentences

Combine numbers, colors, and nouns:

- **Das Auto ist rot.** — The car is red.
- **Ich habe drei Äpfel.** — I have three apples.
- **Zwei Kaffee, bitte.** — Two coffees, please.
- **Die Blume ist gelb.** — The flower is yellow.
- **Der Hund ist schwarz und weiß.** — The dog is black and white.

## Grammar: Predicative Adjectives

When a color word (or any adjective) comes after the verb **sein** (to be), it does **not** change its form:

- Der Tisch ist **rot**. (NOT: roter, rote, rotes)
- Die Tasche ist **blau**. (NOT: blaue)
- Das Haus ist **grün**. (NOT: grünes)

This is called the **predicative** position. The adjective stands alone after the verb, without any ending. This is the easiest way to use German adjectives!
